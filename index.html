ABOUT ME

Hello and Welcome to my webpage,

My name is Jacob Ota, and I am a senior, Computer Science major, at the University of Puget Sound. I was born and raised in Anaheim, California where I attended Canyon High School and where I lived with my Mom, Dad and little sister, Kiana. Growing up I enjoyed hanging out with friends and family, playing sports, and fishing. As a kid I grew up playing a bunch of sports, basketball and soccer were my main two sports before I turned 10, but shortly after those sports I fell in love with the game of baseball. I ended up playing baseball from 10 and continue to play to this day for the University's varsity team. Outside of baseball, I also love going fishing with my dad, and a great fishing spot that he takes me every year is the lakes in Mammoth which is up in Northern California.

A major part of my life outside of school is baseball. Whether it be playing it or watching MLB games, I love just about every part of the game and it has definitely taught me a fair share of life lessons that I will take with me after my playing days are done. The most important and most recent one is the ability to battle adversity, last season for me was a rough one, I sprained my calf and subluxed (partial dislocation) my shoulder twice which in turn led to my season being ended, and over the off-season I got surgery to fix the shoulder so I can swing a bat again. Ever since I got my season-ending shoulder injury the only thing that has been on my mind is "What are the steps I need to take to get back". Even though last season was a tough break, I am working hard to get back and ready for my senior season.

Coming from California to Washington was probably the biggest transition of my life so far, due to the fact of having to live a lot more independent. But so far I feel like I have tackled this transition pretty well so far and I have definitely enjoyed the feeling of living more independently. Coming into college I had planned on being a Exercise Science major, but all that changed due to COVID and made me think a little bit about how I wanted my future to look. So I always had an interest in Computer Science and chose that to be my new plan of action. And since I made the switch I have enjoyed just about every class I have taken. After college I plan on going to grad school for Cybersecurity and my main goal after that is working for the CIA or some government agency.
